One-pan cooking is the weeknight dinner cheat code. One pan means one wash-up, simplified technique, and everything cooking together so flavors meld naturally. These five recipes cover different flavor profiles and work for families, couples, and solo cooking alike.

## The One-Pan Approach: What Makes It Work

Successful one-pan cooking requires attention to cooking time order. Dense, slow-cooking ingredients (root vegetables, thick proteins) go in first or get head starts; quick-cooking ingredients (green vegetables, thin proteins) go in last.

High heat (400–425°F for oven; medium-high for stovetop) creates browning and flavor. Crowding the pan (too many ingredients too close together) creates steaming rather than roasting — leave space between pieces.

## 1. Sheet Pan Lemon Herb Chicken and Vegetables

**Time:** 40 minutes total | **Serves:** 4

**Ingredients:**
- 4 chicken thighs or 2 chicken breasts, bone-in or boneless
- 2 medium zucchini, cut into ½-inch rounds
- 1 red bell pepper, sliced
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- Juice of 1 lemon
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- Salt and pepper

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 425°F (220°C).
2. On a large sheet pan, toss chicken and all vegetables with olive oil, garlic, lemon juice, herbs, salt, and pepper.
3. Arrange chicken in the center, vegetables around it.
4. Roast 30–35 minutes until chicken reaches 165°F internal temperature and vegetables are caramelized.
5. Serve immediately, spooning pan juices over everything.

**Make it better:** Add ¼ cup white wine to the pan before roasting.

## 2. One-Pan Salmon with Asparagus and Potatoes

**Time:** 35 minutes | **Serves:** 2–4

**Ingredients:**
- 4 salmon fillets (6 oz each)
- 1 lb baby potatoes, halved
- 1 bunch asparagus, trimmed
- 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon honey
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- Salt, pepper, dill

**Method:**
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Toss potatoes with 2 tablespoons olive oil, salt, pepper. Spread on sheet pan.
2. Roast potatoes 15 minutes.
3. Push potatoes to sides, add asparagus and salmon.
4. Mix mustard, honey, garlic, remaining olive oil. Brush over salmon.
5. Roast 12–15 more minutes until salmon is cooked through and asparagus is tender-crisp.

## 3. One-Pan Garlic Butter Shrimp Pasta

**Time:** 25 minutes | **Serves:** 4

**Ingredients:**
- 12 oz linguine or spaghetti
- 1 lb large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 4 tablespoons butter, divided
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- ½ cup white wine or chicken broth
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 cup baby spinach
- Red pepper flakes, parsley, parmesan to serve

**Method:**
1. Cook pasta according to package directions. Reserve 1 cup pasta water before draining.
2. In the same pot (or large skillet), melt 2 tablespoons butter over medium-high. Add garlic, cook 1 minute.
3. Add shrimp, cook 2 minutes per side until pink. Remove.
4. Add wine/broth, cherry tomatoes, remaining butter. Simmer 3 minutes.
5. Add pasta, spinach, shrimp, lemon juice. Toss, adding pasta water as needed for sauce consistency.

## 4. One-Pan Spanish Rice and Chicken

**Time:** 40 minutes | **Serves:** 4

**Ingredients:**
- 4 chicken thighs (bone-in)
- 1 cup long-grain white rice
- 1 can (14oz) diced tomatoes
- 1¾ cups chicken broth
- 1 bell pepper, diced
- 1 onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- ½ teaspoon cumin
- Olive oil, salt, pepper

**Method:**
1. Season chicken with paprika, cumin, salt, pepper.
2. Heat oil in large oven-safe skillet over medium-high. Brown chicken 3–4 minutes per side. Remove.
3. Sauté onion, pepper, garlic 3 minutes.
4. Add rice, stir 1 minute. Add tomatoes and broth. Bring to simmer.
5. Nestle chicken back in. Cover, cook on low heat 20–25 minutes until rice absorbs liquid and chicken is cooked through.

## 5. One-Pan Stir-Fry (Infinitely Variable)

**Time:** 20 minutes | **Serves:** 2–4

**Base formula:**
- 1 lb protein (chicken strips, beef, shrimp, tofu)
- 4 cups vegetables (broccoli, snap peas, bell peppers, carrots, bok choy — any combination)
- 3 tablespoons stir-fry sauce

**Simple sauce (whisk together):**
3 tablespoons soy sauce + 2 tablespoons oyster sauce + 1 tablespoon sesame oil + 1 teaspoon cornstarch + 1 teaspoon sugar + 1 teaspoon grated ginger + 2 cloves garlic minced.

**Method:**
1. Heat wok or large skillet to very high heat. Add oil.
2. Cook protein in batches (don't crowd) until browned. Remove.
3. Stir-fry hardest vegetables first (broccoli, carrots), then medium (peppers), then quick (snap peas, spinach).
4. Return protein, add sauce, toss 1–2 minutes until everything is coated and sauce thickens.
5. Serve over rice or noodles.

One-pan meals work best when you own a large, high-quality skillet (12-inch cast iron or stainless) and a large sheet pan with sides. The investment pays off every weeknight.
