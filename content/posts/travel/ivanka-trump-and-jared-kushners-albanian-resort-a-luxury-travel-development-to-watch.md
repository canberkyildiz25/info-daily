When news broke that Ivanka Trump and Jared Kushner were eyeing Albania's stunning coastline for a major luxury resort development, the travel and real estate worlds took notice. Now, as of mid-2026, the project has evolved from whispered rumors into one of the most closely watched hospitality ventures in Southern Europe. Whether you're a luxury travel enthusiast, a real estate investor, or simply curious about Albania's rapid rise as a Mediterranean destination, this development deserves your attention.

## The Big Picture: What We Know About the Project

Ivanka Trump and Jared Kushner's Albanian resort venture is centered along the country's Albanian Riviera, a breathtaking stretch of Ionian coastline that has been drawing comparisons to Croatia and Greece — but at a fraction of the cost and crowd level. Reports indicate the couple has been working through their investment firm and Albanian development partners to secure prime coastal land for what is expected to be a high-end, mixed-use resort complex.

While specific details about the property's exact footprint and amenities continue to emerge, here's what has been reported or confirmed through various outlets:

- **Location:** The southern Albanian coastline, likely near Sarandë or the Ksamil area, known for turquoise waters and UNESCO-adjacent historical sites.
- **Scale:** The project is expected to include luxury hotel accommodations, private villas, wellness facilities, and potentially a marina.
- **Target market:** Ultra-high-net-worth travelers seeking exclusive Mediterranean experiences outside the traditional hotspots.
- **Timeline:** Construction phases are anticipated to extend through 2027 and into 2028, with early components potentially opening sooner.

The project aligns with Kushner's broader portfolio of international real estate ventures, which has expanded significantly since the couple left their roles in the Trump White House in 2021.

## Why Albania? The Rise of an Unlikely Luxury Destination

If Albania seems like an unconventional choice for a celebrity-backed luxury resort, you haven't been paying attention to the country's trajectory. Albania has been one of Europe's fastest-growing tourism markets over the past several years. According to the Albanian Institute of Statistics (INSTAT), the country welcomed over 10 million international visitors in 2024 — a staggering figure for a nation of fewer than 3 million people.

Several factors make Albania attractive for luxury developers:

1. **Untouched coastline:** Unlike much of the Mediterranean, Albania's Riviera still has significant stretches of undeveloped, pristine beachfront land.
2. **Affordable land costs:** Property prices remain dramatically lower than in neighboring Greece, Montenegro, or Italy, offering higher margins for developers.
3. **Government incentives:** The Albanian government has actively courted foreign investment in tourism infrastructure, offering tax breaks and streamlined permitting.
4. **EU candidacy momentum:** Albania's ongoing EU accession process is driving infrastructure improvements, including roads, airports, and utilities along the coast.
5. **Cultural richness:** From the ancient ruins of Butrint (a UNESCO World Heritage Site) to vibrant local cuisine and warm hospitality, the region offers authentic experiences that today's luxury travelers crave.

### A New Corridor for the Global Jet Set

Albania's Riviera has already begun attracting boutique hotels and small luxury operators, but a project of this scale — backed by globally recognized names — could be the tipping point that puts the destination firmly on the luxury travel map. Industry analysts have compared the potential impact to what Aman Resorts did for Montenegro's coast with Aman Sveti Stefan, or how One&Only transformed parts of the Greek islands.

## The Business and Political Dimensions

It would be impossible to discuss this project without acknowledging the complicated intersection of business, politics, and public perception that follows the Trump-Kushner family.

### Kushner's International Investment Strategy

Since leaving government, Jared Kushner has been actively building Affinity Partners, his Miami-based investment firm that has raised substantial capital — reportedly over $3 billion — from sovereign wealth funds and international investors, particularly from the Middle East. The Albanian resort project appears to fit into a broader strategy of identifying undervalued real estate markets in geopolitically strategic locations.

### Criticism and Controversy

The venture has not been without scrutiny. Critics have raised questions about:

- **Potential conflicts of interest** stemming from relationships formed during the couple's time in the White House, particularly with foreign governments and investors.
- **Environmental concerns** about large-scale coastal development in ecologically sensitive areas of the Albanian Riviera.
- **Local impact**, including whether the development will benefit Albanian communities or primarily serve international visitors.

Albanian civil society groups and environmental organizations have called for transparency in the permitting process, urging the government to ensure that development standards align with the country's EU candidacy requirements.

### Supporters' Perspective

On the other hand, supporters argue that a high-profile international investment brings jobs, infrastructure, and global visibility to a region that desperately needs economic development. The Albanian government has publicly welcomed the attention, with officials noting that luxury tourism creates significantly higher per-visitor revenue than budget travel.

## What This Means for Travelers and Investors

### For Luxury Travelers

If you've been looking for a Mediterranean destination that still feels like a discovery rather than a tourist conveyor belt, Albania's Riviera should be on your radar — and not just because of this resort. Here are some practical tips:

- **Visit soon:** The window to experience the Riviera in its relatively unspoiled state is narrowing as development accelerates.
- **Fly into Corfu:** The easiest current access to southern Albania is often through Corfu, Greece, with a short ferry ride to Sarandë.
- **Explore beyond the beach:** Don't miss Gjirokastër (a UNESCO World Heritage city), the Blue Eye spring, and the Llogara Pass for dramatic coastal views.
- **Budget wisely:** Even at the luxury end, Albania remains significantly more affordable than Greece or Italy — for now.

### For Real Estate Investors

Albania's property market has been heating up, and a project like this could accelerate appreciation along the Riviera. However, potential investors should:

- **Conduct thorough due diligence** on Albanian property law, which can be complex and sometimes opaque for foreign buyers.
- **Work with reputable local legal counsel** experienced in international real estate transactions.
- **Monitor the EU accession timeline**, as membership would likely drive significant property value increases but also bring stricter regulations.

## The Bigger Picture for Albanian Tourism

The Trump-Kushner resort is not an isolated phenomenon. It's part of a broader wave of international investment and attention flowing into Albania's tourism sector. Major hotel brands including Marriott and Hilton have been expanding their Albanian presence, and the government has invested heavily in the new Vlorë International Airport, which will dramatically improve access to the southern coast when fully operational.

Albania stands at a crossroads. Done right, luxury development can coexist with environmental preservation and community benefit. Done poorly, it risks repeating the mistakes of overdeveloped Mediterranean coastlines. The Trump-Kushner project, given its visibility and scale, will likely become a bellwether for how Albania navigates this balance.

## Final Thoughts

Whether you view it through the lens of luxury travel, real estate investment, political intrigue, or Albania's national development, the Ivanka Trump and Jared Kushner resort project is undeniably one of the most fascinating hospitality stories unfolding in 2026. The Albanian Riviera is having its moment — and this high-profile bet suggests that the smart money believes the best is yet to come.

Keep this destination on your watch list. By the time the resort opens its doors, Albania's secret may no longer be a secret at all.
