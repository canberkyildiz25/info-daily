Xbox Game Pass — or Microsoft Game Pass as it's now branded — offers one of gaming's best deals: hundreds of games for a monthly subscription, including Microsoft's first-party titles on day one. The problem is the catalog is enormous, constantly changing, and full of titles that range from masterpieces to mediocre. Here's what's actually worth your time in 2026.

## The Essentials You Should Play Immediately

These are the games that justify the subscription on their own.

**Halo Infinite** launched with a rocky reception, but the campaign — available through Game Pass — is genuinely excellent. The open-world structure gives the franchise a fresh feel while the core combat remains some of the best in the genre. If you've never played Halo, this is a solid entry point. If you're a veteran, the return to a more classic tone after Halo 5 is welcome.

**Forza Horizon 5** is the best racing game on any platform. Set in Mexico, it's a massive open world filled with hundreds of cars, race events, and things to discover. The handling model is accessible enough for casual players while retaining enough depth to satisfy enthusiasts. The world itself is gorgeous — the seasonal weather system alone is worth seeing.

**Starfield** is Bethesda's sprawling space RPG, and while it divided opinion at launch, it's grown substantially through updates. The exploration loop — landing on planets, uncovering outposts, building your ship — is deeply satisfying for players who enjoy that style of open-ended RPG.

**Hi-Fi Rush** was one of the genuine surprises of recent years. A rhythm-action game where everything — combat, enemies, environments — pulses to the beat of the soundtrack. It's funny, stylish, and one of the most joyful games Microsoft has published in years.

## Deep Cuts That Deserve More Attention

Beyond the big names, Game Pass is an excellent place to discover games you'd never have bought at full price.

**Pentiment** is a narrative mystery set in 16th-century Bavaria, told through illuminated manuscript art. It sounds niche. It's extraordinary. Developer Obsidian Entertainment — known for RPGs — created something genuinely unlike any other game, and its storytelling is among the best in the medium.

**Tunic** is a puzzle-adventure game styled like a classic Zelda title but hiding a labyrinthine mystery beneath its cute exterior. The game withholds its own instruction manual, giving players pieces of it as they explore — and piecing together the game's rules from cryptic in-game documents is one of gaming's most unique recent experiences.

**Disco Elysium** (available on PC Game Pass) is arguably the greatest RPG ever made. You play as a detective with no memory investigating a murder in a decaying city. There is no combat. Every interaction is a skill check. The writing is brilliant, dark, funny, and politically rich in ways that feel genuinely rare.

**Outer Wilds** is a mystery game about a solar system stuck in a 22-minute time loop. It's impossible to describe in a way that conveys how special it is — the discoveries you make are best experienced blind. If you enjoy exploration and figure-it-out-yourself mysteries, it may become your favorite game.

## For Families and Casual Players

Game Pass has excellent options for households where not everyone is a dedicated gamer.

**Minecraft** is available through the subscription and needs no introduction. Building, survival, creative mode — the game that has captivated children and adults for fifteen years remains excellent.

**A Plague Tale: Innocence** is a third-person action game set in 14th-century France during the Black Death. It follows siblings fleeing both the Inquisition and enormous rat swarms. It's linear, story-focused, and accessible — more like an interactive film than a traditional game. The sequel, Requiem, is equally good and also on Game Pass.

**Psychonauts 2** is one of the most imaginative platform games ever made. You play as a psychic spy traversing the mental landscapes of colorful characters. The creativity of the level design — each mind is a completely different visual world — is extraordinary.

## What's Coming to Game Pass in 2026

Microsoft continues adding major first-party titles on day one. Keep an eye on upcoming additions to the catalog, as the library grows substantially every month. Setting up notifications for new additions — through the Xbox app or Xbox website — ensures you catch things before they leave the catalog.

## How to Get the Most from Game Pass

A few strategies for maximizing your subscription:

1. **Check the "leaving soon" section regularly** — games rotate out, and you don't want to miss something you planned to play
2. **Use the wishlist feature** — it tracks games you want to play and notifies you when they're added
3. **Start games you're uncertain about freely** — with no purchase required, there's no cost to trying something for an hour
4. **Don't let the library overwhelm you** — pick two or three games you're actively playing rather than trying to keep up with everything

## The Bottom Line

Game Pass remains one of gaming's best value propositions. For the cost of a single new release every month, you have access to hundreds of games — including some of the most acclaimed titles of the last decade.

Start with *Hi-Fi Rush* if you want something joyful and immediately accessible. Start with *Outer Wilds* if you want an experience you'll never forget. Start with *Forza Horizon 5* if you want something you can play endlessly without committing to a story.

The subscription pays for itself in the first month.
