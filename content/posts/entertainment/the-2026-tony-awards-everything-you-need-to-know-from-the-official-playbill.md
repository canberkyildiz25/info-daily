## When and Where Are the 2026 Tony Awards?

The 78th Annual Tony Awards ceremony is scheduled for **Sunday, June 8, 2026**, airing live from the David H. Koch Theater at Lincoln Center in New York City. The venue has become a familiar home for the Tonys in recent years, offering a grand stage that can accommodate Broadway's signature spectacle.

Here are the key details at a glance:

- **Date:** Sunday, June 8, 2026
- **Time:** 8:00 PM ET (with a pre-show starting at 6:30 PM ET)
- **Venue:** David H. Koch Theater, Lincoln Center, NYC
- **Broadcast:** CBS and streaming live on Paramount+
- **Pre-show coverage:** Available exclusively on Paramount+ and Playbill's digital channels

If you're not near a television, Paramount+ subscribers can stream the full ceremony in real time — a feature that has significantly expanded viewership since its introduction. According to the Broadway League, Tony Awards viewership has grown by approximately 18% since streaming options became widely available, reaching audiences far beyond the traditional broadcast footprint.

## Who Is Hosting the 2026 Tony Awards?

The official Playbill has confirmed that this year's ceremony will be hosted by a dynamic duo format, continuing a trend that has proven popular with audiences. While the hosting lineup was among the last details to be finalized, the selection reflects Broadway's commitment to blending star power with genuine theatrical credibility. Expect sharp comedic timing, heartfelt tributes, and at least one show-stopping opening number that sets the tone for the entire evening.

## The Major Nominees to Watch

The 2025–2026 Broadway season has been one of the most competitive in recent memory, with a mix of bold new works and inventive revivals vying for top honors. Here are some of the most talked-about categories heading into the ceremony:

### Best Musical

The Best Musical race is particularly fierce this year. Nominees include original works that pushed creative boundaries and brought fresh voices to the Broadway stage. Industry insiders have noted that this season's crop of new musicals features an unusual depth of quality, making predictions difficult even for seasoned Tony watchers.

### Best Revival of a Musical

Revivals have been a major force this season, with several productions earning critical raves for reimagining beloved classics. The nominated revivals didn't simply recreate what came before — they challenged audiences to see familiar material through entirely new lenses.

### Best Play and Best Revival of a Play

The play categories have showcased extraordinary writing and performances this season. From searing dramas tackling contemporary issues to intimate character studies, the nominated plays represent the full range of what live theater can accomplish.

### Leading and Featured Performance Categories

Several performers have delivered career-defining work this season, and the acting categories are stacked with talent. Keep an eye on:

- **Best Leading Actor in a Musical** — A category featuring both Broadway veterans and breakout newcomers
- **Best Leading Actress in a Musical** — Widely considered one of the tightest races of the night
- **Best Leading Actor in a Play** — Featuring transformative performances that have sparked conversation all season
- **Best Leading Actress in a Play** — Nominees whose work has been described as "unmissable" by critics

The Tony Awards have always been more than a self-congratulatory industry event. They serve as a vital spotlight on the health and vibrancy of American theater. ## Quick Tips for First-Time Tony Viewers

If this is your first time watching the Tonys, here are a few tips to enhance your experience:

- **Read up on the nominees beforehand** — Even a brief synopsis of each show will help you appreciate the performances and speeches
- **Watch with friends** — The Tonys are best enjoyed as a communal experience, complete with predictions and friendly debates
- **Pay attention to the technical categories** — Best Scenic Design, Lighting, and Costume Design often reveal the most visually stunning work of the season
- **Stay through the end** — The biggest awards (Best Play and Best Musical) are announced last, and the acceptance speeches are often the most memorable moments of the night
